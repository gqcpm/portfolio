01. About Me

Hello! My name is Gregory Quach, and I’m a Data Scientist with a passion for turning complex data into actionable insights. I’ve had the privilege of working on impactful projects with organizations like San Diego Gas & Electric and Scripps Institution of Oceanography. My work has ranged from developing AI-powered computer vision models for monitoring critical infrastructure, to creating spatial data visualizations that have been used in sustainability policy discussions at the United Nations.

These roles have allowed me to combine machine learning, data analytics, and geospatial analytics to solve complex, real-world problems. Whether it’s reducing human error in powerline inspections or making advanced AI tools more accessible to the public, I’m driven by the challenge of creating solutions that make a tangible impact.

My journey began at the University of California San Diego, where I earned a Bachelor’s degree in Data Science with a minor in Cognitive Science (GPA 3.77). This combination gave me both the technical skills to work with large-scale data and the human-centered approach to design solutions that truly meet user needs.

Here are a few technologies I've been working with recently:

  • Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, Hugging Face)
  • SQL & R
  • ArcGIS & Geospatial Data Mapping
  • Tableau, D3, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Gradio
  • Machine Learning & Computer Vision
  • NLP & LLMs (LangGraph, LlamaIndex)
  • AWS, Docker, Dask, Spark
  • HTML, JavaScript, Figma

03. Some Things I've Built

Featured Project

WaveWatch

A simple Streamlit app that provides surf conditions for any beach using Google Gemini AI. Features include entering any surf beach name, getting AI-generated surf condition summaries, and downloading conditions reports with a clean, intuitive interface.

  • Python
  • Google Gemini AI
  • APIs
  • Fullstack Development

Featured Project

League of Legends Position Prediction

Working along-side Eric Chen, we developed a machine learning model to predict player positions in League of Legends based on post-game data. Used pipelines, column transformers, and a decision tree classifier to achieve 95% accuracy — surpassing the baseline of 52.1%.

  • Python
  • Scikit-learn
  • Pandas
  • Decision Tree Classifier
